UPS

Clinical Trails Logistic Management System

A proof-of-concept application for UPS who sought to explore the viability of a new market, Life Sciences, with the entry point of pharmaceutical clinical trials.

My Roles: UX Research, Information Architect, UX & UI Design, Copywriter & Project Manager

Goals

Build and release to production a fully functional, end-to-end application within five months that would:

  • onboard and service international clients
  • schedule pick-up and delivery of temperature-controlled packaging
  • integrate an ability to work with international third-party shippers
  • print and email shipping labels

Challenges

  • I was brought onto the team at the end of the first month
  • Coding had already begun
  • Users, the account managers, were in Belgium, and I was in New Jersey
  • Needed to move temperature-controlled biologics worldwide, including some countries that utilized third-party services, which complicated the situation

The background image is from a white-boarding session in which we captured the process. "Before" screens – users would access three different systems to generate one sideways label. I knew we could do better.

Strategy

  • Stop development and conduct UX Research
    • have account managers screen share and walk me through their current process, note pain points
  • Whiteboard a user's happy path and then add alternate paths
  • Keep the team focused and mitigate scope creep

Improvements

  • We optimized users' workflow by clearly defining the steps in their process
  • The "order of operations" tabbed layout helps guide users through their tasks
  • Background tint helps define sections and makes input fields more apparent
  • Clearly differentiated two labels to be created

"After" mock-ups – one system. A clean interface that clearly outlines the steps a user must take to schedule the pick-up of a temperature-controlled specimen and the shipping label, vertical and able to check before sending.

The background image shows "boxes & arrows," the steps of a particular flow, a refinement of the white-boarding session. The screen mock-up visualizes the step in the flow below "Defining a Package Type."

Outcome

  • We launched the Clinical Trials Logistics Management System in under five months, optimizing logistics efficiency significantly:
    • reduced client onboarding times by 85%
    • decreased scheduling times for cryogenic content pick-up by 75%
    • improved ambient content pick-up scheduling by 57%
  • Life Science is now a $10 billion division for UPS.
